Action Pros Cons
Fasting according to the Islamically given schedule You get rewarded more Hassanaat than you would in any other month,
and you get more health benefits because the average fast is 17 hours		 You may get hungry more than you ever have
Fasting intermittently You get less demanding hours for fasting, you can use this as a baby step
towards fully fasting, and with the correct schedule given by a professional
you can get just as many health benefits. Also, fasting Mondays and Thursdays
is the Sunnah (way of the Prophet).		 Your schedule could get all over the place and it may be hard eating then
all of a sudden abstaining from food for an entire day abruptly.
Fasting with a specific diet You can still get the feel of fasting as well as most of the beneftis
while still eating anything necessary to battle any illness you may have.
You can also stick to a good diet and have an even more efficient weight loss plan		 You may not get the full reward of fasting, as well as the benefits
